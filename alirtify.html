Designing a Data-Driven UX for News Validation

Overview
Problem How might Alirtify help users validate news more easily?
Time 5 weeks
Team 5 Product Designers
Stakeholders Alirtify CEO
My Role Research, synthesis, UX strategy, UI design
Context

Alirtify is a news aggregator and discussion platform where users interact with articles through likes, reactions, and comments.

The founding idea was that engagement signals could help people identify trustworthy news.

My team was brought in to validate this assumption before building further.

Research goal

Understand how people currently validate news and identify what signals genuinely help them decide whether a story is trustworthy—without telling them what to believe.

Research methodology

User interviews

  • 7 participants
  • Diverse age groups and occupations
  • We assumed that age and profession significantly influence news consumption and trust behaviors, so we intentionally recruited across these dimensions.
Key findings & design decisions

Social validation matters, but who matters more

Research insight

Users care more about whether people they know and trust engaged with an article than abstract global engagement numbers.

UX decision

Surface engagement from a user’s social circle (friends, family, trusted connections) alongside broader engagement metrics to provide more meaningful validation.

Headlines strongly influence trust

Research insight

Clickbait headlines consistently reduced credibility. Many users decided whether to trust or ignore an article based on the headline alone.

UX decision

Introduce AI-generated neutral headline alternatives, allowing users to quickly understand the article without emotional manipulation—reducing misinformation spread driven by sensational headlines.

News source is a primary trust signal

Research insight

Participants relied heavily on news sources to judge credibility, often more than reactions or comments. However, the existing interface underemphasized the source.

UX decision

Visually prioritize the news source in both feed and article views, making credibility checks faster and more intuitive.

Users seek context outside the app

Research insight

Many users cross-checked articles using Google or other platforms to understand broader context or multiple perspectives.

UX decision

Embed related and trending articles directly below the article summary, enabling users to compare perspectives without leaving Alirtify.

Official data builds confidence

Research insight

When topics involved policy, public health, or civic issues, users actively looked for government or verified data sources.

UX decision

Supplement articles with government-backed or verified public datasets where relevant, supporting informed interpretation rather than opinion-driven discussion.

Design direction summary

Instead of relying solely on engagement metrics, the proposed UX supports validation through multiple complementary signals:

  • Trusted social engagement
  • Clear source attribution
  • Neutral headline framing
  • Contextual related articles
  • Verified public data
Outcome

The CEO shared that the engagement provided valuable clarity on validating assumptions early, particularly around research-driven UX decision making.

Reflection

Conducting in-person interviews was initially intimidating, requiring me to adapt my research plan in real time while staying focused on the research objective.

In hindsight, involving journalists and academic researchers earlier could have strengthened:

  • Problem framing
  • Validation criteria
  • Long-term product credibility

Together, these reduce cognitive effort and help users form their own judgments.
